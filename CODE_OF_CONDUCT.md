# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors, maintainers and community members, pledge to make participation in the Humanitarian Connection Protocol (HCP) project a respectful, inclusive and collaborative experience for everyone.

Our goal is to build open humanitarian infrastructure that benefits people worldwide. We believe that respectful collaboration is essential to achieving that mission.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and professional.
- Welcoming new contributors.
- Giving constructive feedback.
- Focusing on technical discussions.
- Respecting different opinions and experiences.
- Prioritizing interoperability, openness and transparency.
- Acting with integrity and good faith.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment or discrimination of any kind.
- Personal attacks or insults.
- Trolling or inflammatory comments.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Intentionally disrupting discussions or collaboration.
- Misrepresenting the project or its contributors.

---

## Project Values

The Humanitarian Connection Protocol is built upon the following principles:

- Humanity
- Neutrality
- Openness
- Transparency
- Collaboration
- Interoperability
- Trust

All participants are expected to uphold these values.

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

They may remove, edit or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests or other contributions that violate these standards.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories
- Discussions
- Pull Requests
- Issues
- Documentation
- Community meetings
- Official communication channels

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers will review every report fairly and objectively.

Depending on the severity of the violation, actions may include:

- Private warning
- Temporary suspension
- Permanent removal from the community
